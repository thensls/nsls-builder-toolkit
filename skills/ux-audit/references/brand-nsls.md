# NSLS Brand Reference

Source: `NSLS_BrandGuidelines_02.27.23.pdf` (Feb 2023). This is the formal honor-society brand — institutional, academic, leadership-focused.

> The NSLS brand and the Society brand are different products with different audiences. Confirm which brand applies before auditing. The Society brand reference is in `brand-society.md`.

## Logos

### Primary wordmark
- NSLS Seal (red ring with crest, "Est. 2001" ribbon, curved "The National Society of Leadership and Success" text)
- Adjacent wordmark: "THE NATIONAL SOCIETY OF LEADERSHIP AND SUCCESS" set in **Brandon Grotesque Bold ALL CAPS**
- Optional tagline: "BUILDING LEADERS WHO MAKE A BETTER WORLD" — in lighter weight underneath

### Variants
- **Seal-only:** when wordmark would be illegible at small sizes
- **Monochrome:** White, Black, Academic Blue, Academic Gray (light variant differs stylistically from dark — never manually swap)
- **Watermark:** Logo Seal or Crest at 20–40% opacity, Soft Light filter, can bleed off the right edge

### Clear space + minimum size
- Clear space: 1× height of wordmark characters on all sides
- Minimum size for full wordmark: 1" / 175px wide
- Smaller than that → use Seal or Crest only

### Misuse (auto-flag as P0/P1 if observed)
- Wrong font on wordmark (anything other than Brandon Grotesque)
- Pairing wordmark with anything other than the full-color seal
- Manually recoloring the seal (e.g., pink, all-black flat)
- Distorting, distressing, outlining, adding effects, or removing elements
- Stretching the seal's proportions
- Using the seal at a size where the curved text becomes illegible

### Ancillary logos (treat as their own brand marks; don't mix with primary wordmark inappropriately)
- Motivational Mondays, Motivational Minutes, Speaker Broadcasts, Leadership Summits, NSLS Pay, Leadership Library Official Selection, Live • Online, Members in the News, Member Spotlight, The Science of Leadership

### Partnership lockups
- Format: NSLS Seal | divider | partner logo
- Partner logo height: 1× height of NSLS seal (logos), or 0.5× if partner is using text wordmark only

## Color palette

### Primary
| Token | Hex | RGB | Pantone |
|-------|-----|-----|---------|
| **Academic Blue** | `#18315A` | 24, 49, 90 | 534 C |
| **Academic Gray** | `#DCDCD7` | 220, 220, 215 | Cool Gray 1 C |
| **Rich Black** | `#000000` | 0, 0, 0 | — |
| **White** | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | — |

### Secondary (collegiate accents)
| Token | Hex | RGB | Pantone |
|-------|-----|-----|---------|
| **Foundation Blue** | `#335F83` | 51, 95, 131 | 7699 C |
| **Advanced Red** | `#C0181B` | 192, 24, 27 | 1805 C |
| **Executive Green** | `#546F6B` | 84, 111, 107 | 5487 C |

### Color rules
- **Avoid using two secondary colors in the same artwork.** Pick one accent per piece.
- **NSLS Blue Gradient** (Academic Blue + Foundation Blue) is for backgrounds + watermarks only — never as text color.
- Secondary colors evoke "excellence, attention, and pride."

### Common color violations to flag
- Pink, orange, or other off-palette accents (e.g., the existing enrollment screen `#c0181b` is on-brand red — but anything else outside the 7 colors above is off-brand)
- Two secondary colors (red + green, red + blue) in one frame
- Blue Gradient used as text color
- Logo or text in colors not from the palette

## Typography

### Display + body
| Use | Font | Weight | Style notes |
|-----|------|--------|-------------|
| **Headers + sub-headers** | Brandon Grotesque | **Bold** | **ALL CAPS only**, -25 kerning, in Academic Blue |
| **Body copy** | Avenir | Book | Sentence case |
| **Presentation/Google Docs fallback** | Montserrat | Bold (header), Regular (body) | Only when Brandon/Avenir aren't available; never elsewhere |

### Hierarchy rules
- **Spacing** between elements is always 1/3 the size of the header
- **Leading** equals the font size
- **Eyebrow** (label above header) = 60% of header size
- **Subheader** = 60% of header size
- **Body copy** = 1/3 of header size

### Color rules per background
| Background | Eyebrow | Header | Subheader | Body |
|------------|---------|--------|-----------|------|
| Academic Blue / Dark | Academic Gray | White | Academic Gray | White |
| Image (must be dark enough) | White | White | White | White |
| White / Light | Foundation Blue | Academic Blue | Foundation Blue | Black |
| Blue Gradient w/ watermark | Academic Gray | White | Academic Gray | White |

### Common typography violations to flag
- **All-caps Brandon Grotesque Bold for body copy** (it's reserved for headers/sub-headers — body should be Avenir Book in sentence case). This is a frequent NSLS-page violation.
- Wrong font (e.g., system fonts, Arial, Helvetica) on a public-facing surface
- Body copy in Brandon Grotesque (it's a display font; reads as shouting at body size)
- Header in something other than Academic Blue (on light backgrounds)
- Mixing brand fonts with non-brand fonts on the same surface
- Montserrat used outside presentations/Google Docs

## Highlight blocks (PRINT / GRAPHICS only — NOT for UI)

The Highlight block system is for marketing collateral, posters, social graphics, and presentation slides. **It is explicitly NOT the guideline for the Brand UI Design System.** Do not flag the absence of these patterns in product UI.

If the artifact IS marketing/print:
- Light backgrounds → red panel + white text
- Dark or medium backgrounds where red would vibrate → white panel + red text
- Bold = 60% of header size; padding height = text height

### Block patterns (marketing)
- **Name + title:** Academic Blue block + name in white; below it a smaller red strip with job title in white
- **Name only:** Advanced Red block + white text; padding = half text height
- **Event blocks:** red "EVENT TYPE" → white "EVENT TITLE" → secondary "By Event Host" + "With Secondary Name" → bottom red strip "EVENT DATE DETAILS"

## Image style

NSLS imagery is "natural" + "human":
- **Natural lighting** — no studio overproduction
- **Natural environments** — campus, conference, workspace, real settings
- **Natural poses** — not stiff, not over-styled
- **Human emotion** — joy, focus, connection
- **Human activity** — work, study, presenting, collaborating
- **Human interaction** — meetings, mentorship, group dynamics

### Common image violations to flag
- Stock photography that reads as generic / staged / overproduced
- Studio-light "isolated subject" photos that don't match the natural-environments mandate
- Imagery without human presence on member/marketing pages (NSLS is a people brand)

## Voice + tone

NSLS voice from the brand identity:
- **Institutional** — academic credibility, accreditation, leadership development
- **Approachable but authoritative** — "Building leaders who make a better world"
- **Specific not motivational** — concrete benefits (scholarships, credit recommendations) outperform generic "live your best life"
- **Trust over scarcity** — university affiliations, accreditation logos, alumni outcomes outperform countdown timers and "limited spots"

### Common voice violations to flag
- E-commerce urgency tactics ("only X left", "expires in 24 hours") — out of brand for NSLS audience
- Overly casual / slangy language on member-facing pages (Society brand can be looser; NSLS cannot)
- Transactional CTAs ("BUY NOW", "GET YOURS") in place of leadership-framed alternatives ("Begin my membership", "Activate my membership")
- Generic motivational copy in place of specific credentials/outcomes language

## What's NOT covered in this reference

## Integration with the heuristic + behavioral audit

When auditing an NSLS-branded artifact, brand findings live in their own severity-rated section alongside SUS / Laws of UX / WCAG findings. Use the same P0–P3 rubric:
- **P0 brand:** logo misuse on a customer-facing surface, off-brand color on a primary CTA, wrong font on the public-facing wordmark
- **P1 brand:** typography rules violated on the primary content surface (e.g., all-caps Brandon Grotesque Bold body bullets), color contrast that breaks the per-background rules, two secondary colors mixed, blue gradient used as text
- **P2 brand:** secondary inconsistencies (eyebrow color wrong, spacing rule violations), Montserrat in a non-presentation context
- **P3 brand:** polish opportunities (typography hierarchy could match the 1/3 spacing rule more strictly)
