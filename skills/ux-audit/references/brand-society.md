# Society Brand Reference

Source: `Society_Brand_Style_Guide_Q42025.pdf` (Q4 2025, Last Updated). This is the modern member-facing brand — fresh, approachable, designed to attract beyond core inducted members.

> The NSLS brand and the Society brand are different products with different audiences. Confirm which brand applies before auditing. The NSLS brand reference is in `brand-nsls.md`.
>
> Society is positioned **"by The NSLS"** — when in doubt, the lockup `Society / By The NSLS` is the canonical identifier.

## Design pillars (the brand's stated guardrails)

The brand defines four pillars; recommendations should align with these, not against them:

1. **Fresh Optimism** — deliver a feeling of a bright future ahead
2. **Approachable Authority** — friendly + trust + credibility, hint of academia
3. **Textured Diversity** — dynamic and adaptable to the wider audience beyond core inducted members
4. **Vision-casting** — allow participants to place themselves in the brand world

These framings are useful audit lenses: a finding that violates "Approachable Authority" by reading too cold or too transactional is a real brand finding.

## Logotype

### Primary
- "Society" set in **Cigars Semibold**, **title case** (capital S, lowercase rest)
- Includes ® mark
- Designed to feel "academic and timeless"

### Lockup (core identifier)
- "Society" wordmark + "By The NSLS" line set in **Inter Regular** below or to the right
- Maintain provided spacing and proportions; **never stretch, reconfigure, or reposition** the lockup elements

### Color combinations (approved)
| Logotype | Background |
|----------|------------|
| Society Black | Society White |
| Society White | Society Black |
| Society White | Illustration |
| Society Black | Grey 5 |
| Society Black | Society Yellow |
| Society White | Photograph (image must be dark enough) |

### Placement
- Preferred: **left-aligned**
- Special occasions: center-aligned

### Misuse (auto-flag as P0/P1 if observed)
- Altering logotype color outside approved combinations (e.g., the seven misuse-page examples explicitly show: pink, multi-color, rotated, modified lockup, white-space-as-holding-shape, compressed/outlined, drop shadow — all are violations)
- Distorting orientation (rotated, vertical)
- Using the defined whitespace as a holding shape (e.g., yellow box around the logotype)
- Compressing, outlining, or altering any letterform components
- Adding effects (drop shadow, glow, etc.)
- Altering the lockup proportions

### Color proportions (mandatory rule)
The brand explicitly defines target proportions for any Society touchpoint, analog or digital:
- **Society White & Grey: 75%**
- **Society Yellow: 20%**
- **Purple, Pink, Green combined: 5%**

A composition that flips this ratio (e.g., 60% yellow background, accent-heavy) is a brand violation even if every individual color is in-palette.

### Common color violations to flag
- Off-palette color anywhere on a Society-branded surface
- Yellow used as primary background covering >20–30% of screen real estate (violates proportions)
- Two accent colors (Purple + Pink, Pink + Green, etc.) used together — accents should appear individually or sparingly
- Black used at >25% (Society is a warm, white-heavy brand; heavy black tilts toward NSLS or off-brand)
- Greys used outside the defined Grey 1–5 tokens (e.g., generic `#888` from a UI library instead of the warm cream-grey scale)

## Typography

### Primary typeface
**Cigars** (serif) — designed to feel academic and timeless. Approved weight: **Medium**.

Use:
- Headlines (h1, h2)
- Sub-headlines (h3) — primarily Cigars Medium

### Secondary typeface
**Inter** (sans-serif) — variable font for screens, tall x-height, computer-screen-optimized. Approved weights: **Regular, Medium, Semibold**.

Use:
- **Inter Regular** — body copy, headlines, sub-headlines, CTAs (lightest, most-used)
- **Inter Medium** — sub-headlines and CTAs
- **Inter Semibold** — emphasis, secondary buttons, smaller titles

### Typography decision tree
| Element | Default font + weight |
|---------|----------------------|
| Logotype | Cigars Semibold |
| Hero headline | Cigars Medium |
| Section heading (h2) | Cigars Medium OR Inter Semibold (designer's choice; consistent within a piece) |
| Sub-headline (h3) | Cigars Medium OR Inter Medium |
| Body copy | Inter Regular |
| CTA / button | Inter Regular or Medium |
| Captions / fine print | Inter Regular |

### Common typography violations to flag
- **Brandon Grotesque used on a Society-branded surface** (Brandon is NSLS — confusion between the two brands is the most common cross-brand violation)
- All-caps body copy (Society uses title case headlines, sentence case body — all-caps is rare in this brand)
- Cigars used for body copy at small sizes (it's a display serif; lower-density screens lose legibility)
- Inter Bold or Inter Black weights (only Regular, Medium, Semibold are approved)
- System fonts (Helvetica, Arial, San Francisco) substituting for Cigars or Inter on a public surface

## Voice + tone

The brand's voice keywords (visible in the brand book hero typography):
- Community
- Career
- Coaching
- Clarity
- Guidance
- Success

Voice character (derived from the design pillars):
- **Fresh, optimistic** — bright future framing, not heavy or formal
- **Approachable + credible** — friendly but trustworthy; hint of academia, not stuffy
- **Specific over generic** — clarity, guidance, concrete career outcomes
- **Inclusive + dynamic** — speaks to the audience beyond core members

### Common voice violations to flag
- NSLS-style institutional phrasing on a Society surface ("THE NATIONAL SOCIETY OF LEADERSHIP AND SUCCESS announces…") — too formal for Society voice
- Generic motivational copy ("unlock your potential", "live your best life") — Society leans specific
- E-commerce urgency tactics — out of brand
- Overly casual / slangy / meme-language — Society is friendly but credible, not internet-casual

## Image / art direction

The brand uses photography combined with illustration / textured backgrounds (visible in the cover and Color section spreads — figure cutouts on yellow with painterly tree backgrounds). Approved photo treatments include:
- High-contrast black-and-white portraits
- Full-color member portraits in natural environments
- Mixed-media compositions: photo subjects on yellow / illustration backgrounds with cutout silhouettes

### Common image violations to flag
- Generic stock photography with no brand treatment / cutout / yellow background
- NSLS-style accreditation-logo-heavy imagery on a Society surface
- Heavy gradients / overlays that don't match the warm-flat-color brand world

## Web reference: Society Web Page V1.6 (2026)

The V1.6 Society marketing-site design is the canonical reference for how Society brand translates to web. Source artifact: `~/Downloads/Society Web Page V1.6 (2).pdf` (also `.png` and `Mobile.pdf` variants). Concrete patterns to apply on any Society-branded web surface:

### Canvas + color usage (observed in V1.6)
- **Cream is the canvas, not white.** Page background is Grey 1 (`#FAF8EE`) or Grey 2 (`#F5F3E9`) throughout — never `#FFFFFF`. Cards sit on cream, not the inverse.
- **Yellow is almost never a background.** It appears as a **tiny 2–3px underline mark** beneath section eyebrow labels ("How Society Works", "About Society", "Member Stories", "Career Coaching", "NSLS Membership"). On the full V1.6 page, total yellow surface area is well under 1%. Use of yellow as a hero panel or button fill is a brand inversion in this design system.
- **Lavender / Society Purple** appears as a sectional background panel — used for the "Our Story" panel and the "How your coach can move you forward" feature cards. It's a feature-block accent, not a primary fill.
- **Society Black `#1E1414`** (warm near-black, NOT `#000000`) for all primary text.

### Buttons (observed in V1.6)
- **Pill-shaped primary CTAs** — fully rounded (border-radius approaching half the button height, ~50px on a 56px-tall button). Not rounded-rectangle.
- Primary CTA = **black fill (`#1E1414`) + white text (`#FFFDF8`)** + Inter Medium or Semibold. Examples in V1.6: "Try Society For Free", "Try Society's AI Coach For Free".
- Secondary / utility CTA = **outlined pill, transparent fill, dark border + dark text.** Example: "Sign In" in the top-right nav.
- No drop shadows. No gradients. No icon-in-button (clean text-only).

### Typography (observed in V1.6)
- **Hero H1:** Cigars Medium, title case, very large (~56–72px desktop). Letter-spacing slightly tight. Line-height ~1.05–1.1. Examples: "Find your path. Find your people.", "Real people, real progress", "Membership means more", "How your coach can move you forward".
- **Section eyebrow label:** Inter Medium, **uppercase**, small (~12–14px), with **2px Society Yellow underline mark** ~24–32px wide, centered above section H2. The label itself reads "About Society" / "Member Stories" / etc. in dark color.
- **Card heading:** Inter Semibold, ~18–20px, title case, Society Black.
- **Body / supporting copy:** Inter Regular, ~14–16px, Society Black or warm dark grey. Generous line-height (~1.5–1.6).
- **Member quote text:** Inter Regular, italic, in a darker cream card. Attribution ("— Cara L.") in Inter Semibold below the quote.

### Cards + imagery (observed in V1.6)
- Card corners are softly rounded (~12–20px radius).
- Member-story photos sit on **painterly textured backgrounds** — pink/purple/yellow watercolor-style halos around cut-out portraits. Not flat color, not full-bleed photography. The texture IS the brand.
- Tiny black **line-drawn icons** (~24–32px, hairline weight) sit above card text — notepad+pencil, person silhouette, group cluster. Never filled, never colored.
- Featured illustration panels (e.g., "Our Story", the three coach cards) use line-drawn character illustrations on the lavender background.

### Layout + spacing (observed in V1.6)
- Generous cream whitespace between sections (~80–120px vertical gaps on desktop).
- Section blocks read top-to-bottom: eyebrow with yellow underline → H2 headline → 1-line subhead → card row.
- Footer: cream background, Society logotype top-left, Inter Regular link list, address/phone/email in a small Inter Regular block.

### Common V1.6 patterns to reuse on member-facing Society surfaces
- Cream canvas + black pill CTAs is the default visual contract.
- Section eyebrow + yellow underline mark is the canonical section-introducer pattern.
- Cigars Medium for any "marketing voice" headline; Inter for everything functional.
- Lavender ("Society Purple" / `#949AE2` tint) is the only large accent panel; reserve for narrative or feature sections.

### V1.6 violations to watch for when porting Society to new surfaces
- Yellow used as background fill on >5% of a screen (violates the 75/20/5 proportions AND the V1.6 web pattern that uses yellow as accent line only).
- Rounded-rectangle (8–12px radius) buttons instead of pills (reads as generic SaaS, not Society).
- Pure `#000000` instead of warm Society Black `#1E1414` (the warmth matters).
- Pure `#FFFFFF` instead of cream `#FAF8EE` (the cream is load-bearing for brand feel).
- Stock photography without the painterly halo treatment (loses the brand world).
- Filled or colored icons instead of hairline line-drawn icons.

## Integration with the heuristic + behavioral audit

When auditing a Society-branded artifact, brand findings live in their own severity-rated section alongside SUS / Laws of UX / WCAG findings. Use the same P0–P3 rubric:
- **P0 brand:** logotype misuse on a customer-facing surface, NSLS Brandon Grotesque on Society surface, color proportions inverted (yellow >50% of UI), wrong font on the wordmark
- **P1 brand:** typography rules violated on the primary content surface, off-palette accent colors, two accents mixed, lockup proportions stretched
- **P2 brand:** secondary inconsistencies (Inter weight outside approved Regular/Medium/Semibold, grey shades not from the Grey 1–5 scale)
- **P3 brand:** polish opportunities (typography hierarchy mixing Cigars and Inter inconsistently within a piece, near-misses on color proportions)
