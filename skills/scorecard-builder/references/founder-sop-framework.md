# Founder Scorecard SOP — the Methodology

*The founder's (Gary's) scorecard methodology, as the skill applies it. Grounded in his Scorecard SOP + circulated example template, reconciled with NSLS practice and research (see `sop-reconciliation-memo.md`). This is the Topgrading "job scorecard" pattern (Bradford Smart): a detailed, outcome-based definition of a role that replaces a vague job description.*

## The core rule

A scorecard defines a role by **the outcomes it must produce and the behaviors it must exhibit** — not the tasks it performs. If it reads like a to-do list, it's wrong. Every line answers "what does 'great in this seat' actually deliver?"

One card = **one double-sided page**. If it doesn't fit, it isn't prioritized enough.

## The four blocks (in order)

### 1. Mission — one sentence

Why the role exists. Not responsibilities — the reason the seat is in the org at all. "This role exists to ___." It should make the altitude of the role obvious.

### 2. Side A — Accountabilities (the "what")

3–6 durable **outcomes**, each with:

- **Weight** — integer %, minimum 10%, summing to exactly 100%. The weight says what matters most; a 5% line doesn't belong on the card.
- **Pass/Fail "Meets" measure** — the concrete, binary bar for the period. At review it's yes/no: was the bar met? Prefer a bonus-grade KPI (one you'd actually stake comp on) with a target and date.
- **Rating** — relative to the measure: **A** = clearly beat the bar (Exceeds) · **B** = met it (Meets) · **C** = missed. ("Predictably meets/exceeds / somewhat short / far short" language is fine.)

Accountabilities are the place to apply the **2×2 portfolio frame** (`portfolio-2x2-framework.md`) for coordination/operating roles, and the drafting guardrails (outcomes-not-tasks, vital few, controllability, quality counterweight) from `research-grounding.md`.

### 3. Core Values (the culture floor)

The 5 NSLS values, each with a **Minimum Acceptable Rating (MAR)** — the canonical rubric sets MAR = 4 (below "Excellent" flags). Scored as a **binary Pass / Flag gate** (NSLS decision — see D1 in `sop-reconciliation-memo.md`). Values are a floor, not a dial: they don't add or subtract a percentage, they gate.

The 5 values:
| Value | Definition |
|---|---|
| Mission Driven | Help the greatest number of people in the most meaningful way. |
| Accountability | Hold ourselves and others responsible for integrity, commitments, and results through courageous honesty. |
| Get it Done, Make it Fun | Deliver results while fostering a positive, fun, and collaborative work environment. |
| Lead with Heart & Service | Lead with empathy, authenticity, and humility through heart-centered, servant leadership. |
| Continuous Improvement & Innovation | Prioritize progress over perfection to improve yourself, our offerings, and the company. |

### 4. Side B — Competencies (the "how")

5–8 behaviors selected from the Topgrading Competency Bank (`competency-bank.md`), each with a MAR set by role. Rating scale: **4 Excellent · 3 Very Good · 2 Good · 1 Weak.** For manager-level roles, a people-development competency (Training/Development/Coaching, Empowerment, or Develops Others) belongs on the card.

## Growth Focus

One development bet for the period — a competency rated below its MAR (or a chosen stretch), plus **one specific observable action** the person will practice. This is the forward-looking, coaching half of the card.

## Cadence

Created at the start of the fiscal year; accountabilities/goals reviewed each quarter; Growth Focus revisited quarterly. The scorecard is the alignment snapshot at T0 — reality (Signal Quick Notes) will drift from it, and that drift is diagnostic, not a scorecard error.

## What the skill deliberately leaves as `[brackets]`

Dates, thresholds, target numbers, and the specific reports/leads named in an accountability. These are set **with the employee** in the alignment conversation. A finished-looking card with invented numbers defeats the point — the bracket is an invitation to align, not a gap.
